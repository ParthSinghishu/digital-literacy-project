##### **Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)**



1. **Never click on suspicious links**
If you get emails/SMS saying “urgent action needed,” verify the sender first instead of clicking directly.
2. **Check website URLs carefully**
Always ensure the site starts with https:// and has no spelling mistakes (e.g., fake banking sites).
3. **Do not share OTPs or passwords with anyone**
Banks, UPI apps, and officials will never ask for your OTP.
4. **Use strong, unique passwords**
Avoid using the same password across apps like email, banking, and social media.
5. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)**
This adds an extra layer of protection to your accounts.
6. **UPI Safety: Never approve unknown payment requests**
Receiving money does NOT require a PIN—only sending money does. If a request asks for a PIN, it’s a scam.
7. **UPI Safety: Verify before you pay**
Always double-check the UPI ID or QR code before making payments, especially in marketplaces or unknown contacts.
8. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for transactions**
Public networks are unsafe for banking or entering sensitive information.
9. **Keep your apps and devices updated**
Updates fix security vulnerabilities that hackers exploit.
10. **Log out from shared devices**

&#x20;   Never leave your accounts open on college computers or cyber cafés.





###### Report Cybercrime Immediately



* National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
* Helpline Number: 1930



Quick reporting increases chances of recovering lost money.
