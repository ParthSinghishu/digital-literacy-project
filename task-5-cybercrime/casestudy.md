###### **Case Study: Phishing Attack on a College Student**



Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted entity.



In this case, a first-year college student received an email that appeared to be from their bank. The email warned that their account would be blocked unless they verified their details immediately. It included a link labeled “Secure Verification.”



Step-by-step, the attack worked like this:



1. The attacker sent a fake email designed to look official (logo, formatting, urgent tone).
2. The student clicked the link, which led to a fake website identical to the bank’s login page.
3. The student entered their username, password, and OTP.
4. The attacker captured this data in real time and used it to access the actual bank account.
5. Within minutes, money was transferred to multiple unknown accounts.



Students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with cybersecurity are common targets because they may not verify links or emails carefully.



The consequences were serious: the student lost a significant amount of money, faced stress and panic, and had to go through a long bank complaint process. Although some amount was later recovered, the incident caused financial and emotional damage.



This case highlights the importance of verifying URLs, avoiding clicking on suspicious links, and never sharing sensitive information online, even if the message appears urgent or official.
